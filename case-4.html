Design process for MerxTeam

Background

MerxTeam supplies resellers to restaurants with kitchen-related products. The company wanted to change its e-commerce platform. With the switch and associated website redesign, they wished to shift more of their business to the web and to give the sales team a better tool for communicating with customers.

Initially, there was only a vague idea of the business and experience goals. Therefore my main contribution to this project was to provide the team with tools for figuring these out and lay the groundwork for a goal-directed design process.

Role: User Experience & facilitation

Process

Discovering goals

To get the team to start thinking about goals from the users' perspectives and establish a user-centric design process, we first set up a workshop where MerxTeam got to create a couple of proto-personas. They were prototypic in the sense of being based solely on the intuitions and experience of the participants. After a moment of sharing, revising, and combining, the team agreed to continue work on four hypothetical users. We concluded the exercise by collaboratively ranking the importance of each proto-persona from business goals that had been discussed and agreed on in previous meetings.

To in greater detail figure out what each proto-persona would require from the website, our next step was to create an impact map. From the motivations and needs of each proto-persona, we reasoned about what would help them to realize these and contribute to identified business goals.

These methods helped to create a lean UX design process focused on rapidly getting a minimal viable product (MVP) out for continued improvement.

Project planning

The outcomes of the workshops were summarized in a story map - breaking down insights into user stories and prioritizing them for the MVP. The story map was also reworked into wireframes for the team to evaluate and to get a better view of what we would be building.
