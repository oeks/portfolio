Store return app for Lindex

Brief

The retailer Lindex wanted to improve the process for returning orders placed online in their stores by developing a web app that could be run both on a tablet and in their current Point of Sale (POS) system.

Role: User Experience and User Interface design

Process

Research

Stakeholder interviews and a literature review of design considerations for POS systems contributed to defining basic requirements and constraints for the design. The existing return process was investigated by visiting stores and interviewing employees about their experiences.

Design

Early sketches of potential solutions were created with pen and paper (and in the wireframing tool Balsamiq as displayed above) and discussed together with store employees and developers in an effort to receive quick and honest feedback. The final design was made in Adobe XD and shared with developers.

Evaluation and follow-up

The app was first released to a small group of "super users" who were encouraged to report all issues and questions in a closed discussion forum. The app was generally well-received as it made the return process significantly easier and less time-consuming. The largest change after development was adding more friction to the return process as the perceived ease of processing returns could make users feel a bit anxious.
