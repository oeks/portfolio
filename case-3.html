Conversion rate optimization for Lindex

Background

The user experience undoubtedly plays a big part in how successfully a website manages to convert visitors into paying and returning customers. At Lindex, I acted as a UX resource in a Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) constellation where A/B-testing as a method was used to improve the conversion rates in various ways.

Role: User Experience, User Interface Design, and development

Process

Research and analysis

Heuristic evaluations and user feedback ongoingly sparked a lot of ideas on improvements to the user experience. These ideas could be explored early together with data scientists by diving into droves of behavioral data such as click maps, funnels, and session recordings. We also had tools for surveying visitors through the website or through e-mails to get our hands on attitudinal data. Hypotheses could then be expressed as user stories and prioritized in a separate backlog by the group's collective confidence in them, ease of implementation, and estimated impact.

Design

Sketches and prototypes of suggested changes were shared and refined within the group. Minor changes were coded and reviewed directly in the a/b-testing tool (Google Optimize).

Evaluation

Sales-related KPIs were tracked between test versions as all these changes had the business goal of positively affecting conversion rates. These findings needed to occasionally be supplemented by attitudinal data to ensure that users also perceived these changes to be improving their experiences. It was not in the group's interest to compromise the user experience over short-time winnings. Test results were subsequently shared with the larger team and changes were recommended for development if the hypothesis appeared to hold.

Learnings

A/B-testing appears to be an easily graspable concept for a wider organization as the method delivers concrete results. As such it could be a valuable tool for instilling a data-driven culture. On the other hand, it became important to contextualize the data by relating it to the overarching product vision and strategy.
